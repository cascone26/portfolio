# Google Business Profile — BuiltSimple Setup Guide

**Time required:** 30-45 minutes
**Why it matters:** Local search. When a KC business owner searches "web designer Kansas City" on Google Maps, this is how you show up. Free, high intent, takes one sitting.

---

## Step 1 — Claim the Profile

1. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Search "BuiltSimple" — if a profile exists, claim it. If not, click **Add your business**
4. Business name: **BuiltSimple**

---

## Step 2 — Fill Out Every Field

### Category
- **Primary:** Web Designer
- **Secondary:** Internet Marketing Service, Graphic Designer (optional)

### Address
- If you work from home: use a service area instead of a physical address
- Service area: **Kansas City, MO** + radius (suggest 50 miles to cover KC metro, Lawrence, Leavenworth, Olathe)

### Contact
- Website: `https://builtsimple.dev`
- Phone: your number (optional but recommended — it's a trust signal)

### Hours
- Set realistic hours or use "By appointment"
- Don't leave blank — GBP penalizes missing fields

### Description (use this or edit it)
```
BuiltSimple builds fast, affordable websites for small businesses in Kansas City.
We specialize in restaurants, contractors, salons, and local service businesses.
No monthly fees, no hidden costs — just a site that works and gets you found on Google.
Most projects ship in 1-2 weeks.
```
*(Under 750 characters. Keep it local-keyword-rich: "Kansas City" appears twice.)*

---

## Step 3 — Upload Photos

Google ranks profiles with photos higher. Upload:

1. **Logo** — use the BuiltSimple logo from `/public/logo.png`
2. **Cover photo** — screenshot of builtsimple.dev homepage (1024x576 minimum)
3. **Work samples** — 3-5 screenshots of client sites or portfolio mockups
4. **Team photo** — even a solo headshot helps (personal = trustworthy)

**Minimum:** Logo + cover + 2 work samples before going live.

---

## Step 4 — First Google Post

Posts show in search results and signal the profile is active. Do one immediately after setup.

**Suggested first post:**
```
We build websites for small businesses in Kansas City — restaurants, contractors,
salons, and more. Fast turnaround, flat pricing, no monthly fees.

See our work: builtsimple.dev
```
Post type: **What's new**
Add a screenshot of the portfolio page as the image.

---

## Step 5 — Get 5 Reviews Fast

Reviews are the biggest ranking factor. You need at least 5 to show the star rating.

**Who to ask first:**
1. Anyone you've done paid work for
2. Former colleagues or clients who can speak to your web work
3. Anyone who used the free mockup offer (even if they didn't buy)

**How to ask:**
1. In GBP dashboard: **Get more reviews** → copy the short review link
2. Text or email directly: "Hey [name], could you leave me a Google review? Here's the link: [link]. Takes 2 minutes."
3. Add the link to your email signature after setup

**Do not:** Offer incentives for reviews. Google will remove them.

---

## Step 6 — Set a Recurring Reminder

Post once a week for the first month. After that, 2x/month is enough to stay active.

Post ideas:
- New client site launched (screenshot + brief description)
- Tip for small business owners ("3 things your website needs before running ads")
- Before/after of a redesign
- Link to a new blog post

---

## Tracking

After 30 days, check **GBP Insights:**
- How many searches found you
- How many clicked your website
- How many called

Target at 30 days: 50+ views, 5+ website clicks. If lower, add more photos and posts.
