## Article Structure

### 1. Hook: The $2,000 Mistake
**Key Points:**
- Story of a local business owner who spent $2,000 on a website that doesn't rank
- The one thing they didn't understand: keywords
- Preview that this is fixable and not complicated

### 2. What Are Keywords? (The Simple Answer)
**Key Points:**
- Keywords = the words people type into Google when looking for businesses like yours
- Example: "plumber near me", "best pizza Kansas City", "wedding photographer"
- Your website needs to "speak Google's language"
- It's not magic, it's just matching what people search for

### 3. Why Keywords Matter for Your Business
**Key Points:**
- Google shows websites that best match what people search for
- Without the right keywords, you're invisible even if you're the best business
- Real example: Two competing businesses, one ranks #1, one doesn't show up at all
- The difference: understanding what customers actually search for vs. what you think they search for

### 4. The Types of Keywords That Matter Most
**Key Points:**
- **Location keywords**: "Kansas City plumber", "barber shop downtown"
- **Service keywords**: "emergency plumbing", "men's haircut", "wedding cake"
- **Problem keywords**: "clogged drain", "bad haircut fix", "last minute catering"
- **Buying keywords**: "hire a plumber", "book appointment", "get quote"

### 5. How to Find Your Keywords (The Free Way)
**Key Points:**
- Start with Google autocomplete - type your service and see what appears
- Check Google's "People also ask" section
- Look at what your competitors rank for (free tools to do this)
- Ask your customers: "How did you find me?" / "What did you Google?"
- Use Google Business Profile insights to see actual search terms

### 6. Common Keyword Mistakes Small Businesses Make
**Key Points:**
- Using industry jargon instead of customer language
- Targeting keywords that are too broad ("restaurant" vs "Italian restaurant Kansas City")
- Ignoring location-based searches
- Forgetting about voice search ("near me" searches)
- Not updating keywords when services change

### 7. Where to Put Your Keywords
**Key Points:**
- Page titles (most important)
- Headers and subheaders
- Throughout your content (naturally, not stuffed)
- Image file names and alt text
- Meta descriptions
- Google Business Profile

### 8. Quick Wins: 3 Things You Can Do Today
**Key Points:**
1. Update your homepage title to include your main keyword
2. Add location-specific keywords to your contact page
3. Write service pages that match how customers search
- Each action takes 10 minutes or less
- Will make a difference in 2-4 weeks
