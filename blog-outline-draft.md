# Blog Post Outline: School Website Design

**Target Keyword:** "school website design"
**Secondary Keywords:** "private school website," "education website design," "school website best practices"
**Target Audience:** School administrators, private school owners, education entrepreneurs
**Estimated Word Count:** 750-800 words

## Title
"School Website Design: 7 Essential Features That Convert Parents Into Enrolled Students"

## Meta Description
"Learn the essential school website design features that convert visitors into enrolled students. Expert tips for private schools, academies, and education businesses."

## Section Outline

### 1. Introduction (100 words)
- Hook: "Your school's website is often a parent's first impression of your educational program"
- Problem: Most school websites focus on administration needs, not parent conversion
- Promise: 7 specific features that turn website visitors into enrolled students
- Context: 2026 enrollment challenges for private schools

### 2. Clear Value Proposition Above the Fold (120 words)
- What makes your school different in 3 seconds or less
- Academic achievements, unique programs, or teaching philosophy
- Avoid education jargon parents don't understand
- Example: "College prep with 98% acceptance rate" vs "Rigorous academic environment"

### 3. Streamlined Admissions Process (110 words)
- Online application forms that actually work on mobile
- Virtual tour scheduling integration
- Clear next steps after initial inquiry
- Admission timeline and requirements upfront
- Remove friction from the enrollment funnel

### 4. Social Proof That Builds Trust (100 words)
- Parent testimonials with photos and full names
- Student achievement stories and college acceptances
- Faculty credentials prominently displayed
- Accreditation badges and awards
- Real metrics: class sizes, teacher-to-student ratios

### 5. Transparent Pricing and Financial Information (90 words)
- Tuition costs clearly stated (don't make parents dig)
- Financial aid and scholarship information
- Payment plan options
- Additional fees breakdown
- ROI messaging around college preparation

### 6. Mobile-First Parent Communication (80 words)
- Parent portal access
- School calendar integration
- Emergency notification systems
- Mobile-responsive design for busy parents
- Contact information that works (click-to-call)

### 7. Local SEO for Geographic Targeting (80 words)
- Location-specific landing pages
- "Best private schools in [city]" keyword optimization
- Google My Business integration
- Local community involvement showcase
- Directions and campus accessibility information

### 8. Conclusion: Implementation Strategy (70 words)
- Start with user experience audit from parent perspective
- Focus on enrollment conversion before fancy features
- Test website on mobile devices
- Regular updates and maintenance importance
- Call-to-action: Professional school website consultation

## Key Points Per Section
- **Focus on conversion:** Every element should move parents toward enrollment
- **Trust building:** Schools require higher trust than typical businesses
- **Mobile priority:** Parents research schools on phones during work breaks
- **Local focus:** Private schools compete within geographic boundaries
- **Transparency:** Pricing and admission requirements reduce inquiry friction
